## Why Regression Testing Matters

Regression testing verifies that software changes do not compromise existing functionalities. It prevents new features or updates from inadvertently causing issues, ensuring consistent, reliable software.

### Key Benefits:

- **Consistency:** Confirms stability and usability after updates.
- **Risk Management:** Mitigates issues caused by hidden dependencies.
- **Quality Control:** Enables seamless user experiences.
- **Cost Efficiency:** Prevents expensive post-deployment fixes.

## Limitations of Traditional Regression Testing

Conventional regression testing methodologies struggle to meet modern software demands due to their:

- **Time Consumption:** Manual writing and maintenance of test scripts delay delivery.
- **High Costs:** Skilled testers expend hours on repetitive tasks.
- **Complex Scenarios:** Covering interconnected app environments increases the difficulty.
- **Lack of Flexibility:** Static methods fail in dynamic settings.
- **Data Overload:** Oversights arise from redundant and irrelevant test cases.
